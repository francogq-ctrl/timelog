# Team announcement — Friday Recaps

Drafted to post in `#general` the morning of the first real broadcast (the
Friday after soft-launch is verified). Sets expectations so the recaps don't
arrive cold.

---

## Option A — short and casual

> **🔁 Heads up: weekly recaps start tonight**
>
> Starting tonight at 10pm, every Friday you'll get two things in Slack:
>
> 1. A *personal DM* from `@TimeLog Reminder` with your week — hours, billable %, top clients, streak, and how you're trending vs. your own 4-week average.
> 2. A *team post* right here in `#general` with the agency scoreboard — capacity utilization, % time per client, training, billable mix.
>
> Why we're doing this: your tracking already powers a lot of decisions (pricing, hiring, workload balance). This is us *closing the loop* and showing you what your data says back.
>
> The team post is aggregate only — no individual breakdowns, no rankings, nothing that compares one of us to another.
>
> Questions? DM me. 🙏

---

## Option B — a bit more context (longer)

> **🔁 Closing the loop on time tracking**
>
> Most of you have been tracking your hours in Timelog every day, and it's working — we have real data now to price clients, plan capacity, and protect the team from burnout. Thank you for doing it.
>
> The problem is: until now, that data has only flowed *up* (managers, leadership). You haven't seen anything come back. That changes tonight.
>
> Starting tonight at 10pm, every Friday you'll get two things in Slack:
>
> *1. A personal DM* with your week:
> • Hours logged + billable mix
> • Your top clients
> • Your streak
> • How you're trending vs. your own 4-week average
> • A short reminder of *why* this matters that rotates each week
>
> *2. A team post in this channel* with the agency scoreboard:
> • Capacity utilization (e.g. "we used 95% of 400h available")
> • % time by client
> • % invested in training
> • Overall billable mix
>
> A few important things about the team post:
> • It's *aggregate only*. No individual hours, no rankings, no comparisons.
> • The goal isn't pressure — it's so we all see the same scoreboard and understand how our work translates into the business.
> • Tasks differ in weight. More hours doesn't mean more value, and we won't read the data that way.
>
> Soft-launched it last week with just Michael and me to verify the numbers. They check out.
>
> Questions, edits, or things you'd like to see in future recaps? DM me. 🙏

---

## Recommendation

Use **Option A** if Michael's tone with the team is normally short and informal. Use **Option B** if there's been any tension or pushback on tracking before — the explicit "no rankings, tasks differ in weight" line addresses that head-on.

Either way, post it the morning *of* the first real recap so it's fresh when the messages drop.
